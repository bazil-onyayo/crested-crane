National Status

The Crested Crane is the national bird of the African Nation of Uganda. It appears on her flag and her coat of arms. In 1893, the then governor of Uganda, Sir Frederick Jackson chose this bird as a symbol on the Union Jack.

Scientific Name

It is called Balearica Regulorum Gibbericeps. Balearica is the genus and Regulorum is the species. The crested crane is a sub-species called Gibbericeps native to Uganda and the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

Physical Appearance

The crested crane is a tall bird averaging a height of 3 feet and a weight of 3.5 kilograms. It's head has a velvet black forehead, a yellowish [golden] crown, red inflatable throat pouches and white sides.

Food And Diet

The Crested crane is omnivorous which means that it can feed on both animal and plants. Seeds, grass, insects, worms, rats, small fish and even snakes. But it prefers to eat the seeds of grasses and sedges.

Natural Habitant

The crested crane only lives in areas of Eastern and Southern Africa. It is generally found in dry and open areas but loves to nest around wet areas like river banks and wetlands.

Social Habits

The Crested Crane has a breeding display that involves an elaborate dance with various jumping and bows. Unlike other birds, it is known to dance year-round including non-breeding periods.
The crested crane is a monogamous creature, having one partner for all their lives.

Incubation And Lifespan

The crested crane typically lays 2 - 4 eggs, and the eggs are ready to hatch in about 30 days.
A crested crane can live for an average of 22 years. Crested craneS reach maturity in 3 years, which given their long life-span is promising for getting their population back to normal

Conservation Status

The grey crowned crane is listed by the International Union for the Conservation of Nature (IUCN Redlist) as endangered. Recent studies indicate that the population of these birds keeps decreasing and they could soon become critically endangered. Current global populations is around 22,000 mature birds.
